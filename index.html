What this page cannot show you

This page tests what your browser reveals to any website you visit. But real-world tracking and surveillance go far beyond browser APIs.

  • ISP logging — Your ISP logs every domain you visit. A VPN hides this from your ISP but your VPN provider sees it instead.
  • DNS leaks — DNS queries may leak to your ISP even when using a VPN, unless you use DNS-over-HTTPS or DNS-over-TLS.
  • Traffic analysis — Packet sizes and timing patterns can reveal what you're doing even through encrypted connections.
  • Email & document metadata — Tracking pixels in emails and metadata in files (EXIF in photos, author fields in documents) leak information outside the browser.
  • Cross-site tracking — Third-party cookies, tracking pixels, and login fingerprinting across sites cannot be fully demonstrated from a single page.
  • Network-level surveillance — Router, corporate firewall, or national-level monitoring operates below anything a browser can detect.

Privacy tips & mitigations

  • Use a trusted VPN or privacy proxy to mask your IP, ASN, and geolocation.
  • Disable third-party cookies and limit cross-site tracking protections in your browser settings.
  • Turn off WebRTC or restrict local IP sharing in your browser to reduce leak surface.
  • Use privacy-focused extensions that block fingerprinting scripts and limit hardware disclosures.
  • Use Tor Browser to resist canvas, WebGL, and AudioContext fingerprinting — it normalizes these outputs.
  • Use DNS-over-HTTPS or DNS-over-TLS to prevent DNS leaks.
  • Strip metadata from files before sharing (EXIF data in photos, author fields in documents).
  • A VPN shifts trust from your ISP to the VPN provider — it does not eliminate logging.
